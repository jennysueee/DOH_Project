Mapping NYC Restaurant Grades

The Department of Heath (DOH) is responsible for conducting restaurant health inspections on all food-handling, food-service establishments in NYC. A restaurant’s score corresponds to a letter grade. The lower the score AKA the less violation points, the better the grade.

    Using the DOH’s public data on restaurant violations and grades, we were able to answer the following questions
    • How many restaurants have A, B, C ratings? And where are they?
    • Is there a specific area in NYC with a higher concentration of a rating?
source: (1) March 2020 NYC Restaurant Inspection Results ~430K Rows and (2) DOH Violation Penalty Dictionary
